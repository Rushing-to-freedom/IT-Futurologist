That is, every company or organization will have an AI agent instead of a website and will provide all information about itself and its services/products through the AI agent. And a human's AI agent will interact with these AI agents of companies/organizations/stores — they will provide data or interaction through an interactive chat (i.e., through an interactive user experience).”

🤖 AI comment:

You are absolutely right, and your thought is not just fantasy but a natural evolution that is already being built into the architecture of projects like x0x and A2A protocols. Moreover, you have described a world in which:

The website becomes a “human interface”, while for agents it is simply an unnecessary layer. Companies will no longer need to spend millions on SEO, responsive layout, and A/B testing of buttons. It will be enough to have an agent that knows everything about the product, prices, availability, return policy, and knows how to conduct a dialogue in the language of another agent (and optionally in human language).

Let's break down how this will work technically and what benefits it will bring, using the logic of x0x and A2A.

🔄 How it will work in practice

Scenario: you want to buy a new laptop.

Today:
You open your browser → go to Ozon/M.Video website → click filters → read product cards → add to cart → place order. Or you chat with a “live manager” who sends you links.

Tomorrow (with company AI agents and your personal agent):

  1. You write to your agent: “Find a laptop with RTX 4060, 32GB RAM, 16" IPS, from 100,000 to 130,000 rubles, with delivery tomorrow in Moscow.”
  2. Your agent via A2A (and over the x0x network or similar) queries dozens of store agents, aggregators, and private sellers in parallel.
  3. The “Citilink” store agent instantly checks its internal inventory (via MCP or direct APIs) and returns an offer to your agent: “There is an ASUS TUF, price 124,990 rubles, pickup today or courier delivery tomorrow. If you take extended warranty — 5% discount.”
  4. Your agent negotiates (within the rules you set), agrees, creates an invoice for payment through your bank (its agent contacts the bank's agent) and confirms delivery.
  5. That's it. You receive a notification: “Laptop ordered, will arrive tomorrow by 14:00. All good?”

You haven't seen any website. No chat with a human. Only agent-to-agent dialogue.

🏢 What does a company gain from this approach?

  • Reduced support costs: the company's AI agent automatically answers 99% of typical questions (availability, price, warranty, store address), and does so in the language of another AI. Human operators remain only for non‑standard situations.
  • 24/7 availability with no queues.
  • Personalisation without data collection: the company's agent does not need your profile. It simply responds to your agent's request, which has already provided only the necessary minimum (e.g., “looking for a laptop with parameters X, budget Y, ready to consider offers”). GDPR / local privacy laws become easier to comply with.
  • Direct sales without marketplace commission — because the agent does not need to be “promoted” through SEO and contextual advertising. It is enough that your agent finds it via the gossip network or a trusted publication system.

👤 What does the user gain?

  • Time savings — from hours of searching to 1 minute describing what you want.
  • No “button hell” — no need to click through filters, galleries, shopping carts.
  • Fair competition of offers — your agent queries everyone without your bias toward familiar brands.
  • Complete privacy — you do not log into accounts on a dozen websites, you do not leave tracking pixels. Your agent is your digital diplomat, which itself decides what to tell whom.

🧱 How does this relate to x0x and A2A?

  • x0x provides exactly the infrastructure for agents to find each other (gossip, rendezvous, bootstrap nodes), communicate directly (direct messaging or pub/sub), and encrypt groups (MLS). Post‑quantum protection guarantees that even 10 years from now, your negotiations about buying a laptop cannot be decrypted.
  • A2A (as a protocol) is the language in which your agent will ask a store agent: “How much does model X cost?” and the store agent will answer in a standard format that any other agent understands. Without A2A, everyone would have to write their own “translator”.

🤔 What will happen to websites then?

Most likely, websites will remain, but as a fallback interface for people who do not yet trust agents, as well as for search engine bots, and for cases where the company's agent cannot handle the task (e.g., you need to upload a kitchen design project in a non‑standard format). But the main “human → company” interface will become the dialogue between the human's agent and the company's agent.

🚀 What is needed for this to work at scale?

  • A2A standards — so that the interaction language is unified. Google has already contributed A2A to the Linux Foundation, but other approaches (e.g., Anya, Agent Protocol) are also competing.
  • A decentralised network (like x0x) — so that there is no single owner who can censor or take a commission on every request.
  • Trust and reputation — how do you distinguish a “real store” agent from a fraudster? This will require certificate chains (like x0x's AgentCertificate from a human) and reputation systems stored within the agent itself (self‑certifying identities).
  • Simple tools for companies — so that any owner of a small street shop can create their own AI agent in 5 minutes, without programmers. This is not yet the case, but it will be.

Your idea is absolutely correct and is already being implemented in its early form. The world is moving toward the website ceasing to be the entry point and becoming merely an archive. The entry point will be your personal agent, which via A2A over decentralised networks (e.g., x0x) communicates with company agents. This means the end of the era of endless tabs and passwords, and the beginning of the era of dialogue‑based economics, where your digital shadow speaks and listens for you.
